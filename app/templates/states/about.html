Normal Range

Why it is Important

Hive Temperature

90℉ - 95℉

During construction, this temperature keeps the wax firm but also workable.

Hive Humidity

50% - 60%

If the humidity is too high or low, there is an increased chance of your eggs not hatching.

To keep the hive dry lean it forward slightly so that rain water can drain out.

Make sure the hive is ventilated! Moisture can build up in the hive from the nector dehydrating and your bees evaporating water.

Hive Weight

+-10 oz. difference in each frame in a super.

Your bees will start in the middle frames and work their way outwards in each super.

When the frames in one super become the same weight (+-10 oz.) The super is full of honey.

Some of your supers will need to be left alone to feed your bees, but others can have their honey removed.

Hive Light

0 - 10,000 lumens

The hive light will help determine what time of day it is and the weather.

Bee Population

20,000 - 80,000

Your bee population will depend on the time of year.

A hive consists of 20,000 - 30,000 bees in the winter, and over 60,000 - 80,000 bees in the summer.

The population measured is only the number bees at the enterance of your hive.

There may be a large number, especially if the bees need to fan out the hive.

If there are no bees or very little bees, that could cause an alert.

Bee Frequencies

Good

Your queen bee emits a different frequency than the worker bees.

This will help you keep track of where your queen is and how many queens you have.
